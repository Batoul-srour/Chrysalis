    Our News

    Improved cardiovascular health:

    Regular exercise strengthens the heart and improves blood flow. This helps reduce the risk of heart disease, lowers blood pressure, and improves cholesterol levels, making your heart and blood vessels work better.

    Weightlifting:

    Weightlifting builds muscle strength by using weights. It helps increase muscle size, makes bones stronger, boosts metabolism, and improves overall body strength and shape.

    2-3 liters:

    Drinking 2-3 liters of water daily keeps your body hydrated. It helps with digestion, keeps your skin healthy, and ensures your body functions properly. Staying hydrated prevents headaches, fatigue, and dizziness.

    Proteins:

    Proteins are essential for repairing and building muscles after exercise. They help your muscles recover and grow stronger. Eating enough protein supports overall health and muscle maintenance.

    150 minutes:

    Exercising for at least 150 minutes a week improves your heart health, strengthens muscles and bones, and boosts your mood. Activities like walking, cycling, or swimming count towards this goal.

    Prevent injury:

    Cooling down after exercise helps prevent injuries. It gradually lowers your heart rate and relaxes your muscles, reducing soreness and stiffness. Stretching during cool-down improves flexibility and aids recovery.
